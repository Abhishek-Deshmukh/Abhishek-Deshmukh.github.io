Why is inductive capacitance taken in parallel to the inductor?

The inductive capacitance shown by inductors comes under of phenomenon of parasitic elements(specifically parasitic capacitance).

When two electric conductors at different voltages are kept close to one other they tend to apply electric field on one anoter leading to charge being stored on them this effect is called as parasitic capacitance.

Parasitic Capacitance is a big problem when it comes to high frequency circuits.

The parasitic capacitance between the turns of an inductor or other wound component is often described as self-capacitance. However, the term self-capacitance more correctly refers to a different phenomenon; the capacitance of a conductive object without reference to another object.

Above is my best bet as to why the inductive capacitance is taken in parallel to the inductance.
